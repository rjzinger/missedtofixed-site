Sample · ages 3–5 · God's peace at bedtime

When Sam Couldn't Sleep

Sam lay very still in the dark. The house made little creaks. A branch tapped the window. His heart went thump, thump, thump.

“Mama,” he called softly, “my heart is going too fast.”

Mama sat on the edge of his bed. “Let's give those worries to God,” she said. “He's big enough to hold every one.”

So Sam whispered his worries, one by one — the creaks, the dark, the tapping branch. And with each one, he imagined handing it gently up to God, like passing a heavy backpack to someone strong.

His heart began to slow. Thump… thump… thump.

“God's got them now,” said Mama. “You can rest.”

And Sam did. The creaks kept creaking, but they didn't worry him anymore. Some One bigger was awake, holding it all.

“In peace I will lie down and sleep, for you alone, Lord, make me dwell in safety. — Psalm 4:8”
